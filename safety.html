Safety and Concerns

When dealing about AI Robots, safety is our primary concern. We adhere stricly to the Rules of Robotics posed by Isaac Asimov, which are as follows:

  1. A robot may not injure a human being or, through inaction, allow a human being to come to harm.
  2. A robot must obey orders given it by human beings except where such orders would conflict with the First Law.
  3. A robot must protect its own existence as long as such protection does not conflict with the First or Second Law.

Some concerns that our customers may have are:

  • Lack of human interaction leading to patients feeling depressed, worsening their health
  • Malfunction or loss of power during a critical time (ex. patient has a stroke)
  • Lack of adaptability the human doctors/nurses have (ex. response to natural catastrophes, fires, lockdown procedures)
  • Takes up jobs, therefore increasing the unemployment rate
  • Ai Robots are very costly
  • Some patients might have issues being treated by a robot and refuse to cooperate, disparity between quality of human and robot doctors
